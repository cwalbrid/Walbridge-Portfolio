A Bit About Me

I'd describe myself as curious, integrative, and collaborative. I like to know how things work, I like to connect the dots between ideas/concepts, and I love to work with other people.

During my undergrad at the University of Michigan, I was a bit of a dabbler:

  1. I wrote and narrated an ad for an Ann Arbor/Detroit transit program
  2. I facilitated dialogues on race, gender, and sexuality
  3. I taught creative writing to male prison inmates
In my spare time, I helped put on TEDx events, went for runs in the Nichols Arboretum, and even played a bit of IQA Quidditch!

I originally considered attending a tech bootcamp in Chicago called Startup Institute. Going through the application process for that program was my first exposure to the tech scene, and I was struck by how kind, knowledgable, and passionate the folks in tech are. Logistics for attending that program didn't work out, but it whet my appetite for pivoting to a career in tech.

I didn't have to look far to find to find a program of comparable quality in my own backyard. After reading lots of alumni testimonials and attending Grand Circus workshop + demo day events, I'm confident that Grand Circus is a solid choice.
